The Problem With Eye Creams

Most eye creams cost $60-$120. They contain good ingredients. But they share one fundamental problem: they sit on top of your skin.

The under-eye area has the thinnest skin on your body — just 0.5mm. Eye creams are designed to moisturize the surface. The active ingredients (peptides, retinol, vitamin C) mostly evaporate before they penetrate deep enough to make a difference.

How Patches Are Different

Hydrogel patches create an occlusive seal against the skin. Nothing evaporates. The concentrated actives are pushed directly into the dermis over 20-30 minutes. Clinical studies show hydrogel delivery achieves 5-8x deeper penetration than topical creams.
